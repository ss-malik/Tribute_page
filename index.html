Here's a time line of Major Dhyan Chand's life:
  • Major Dhyan Chand was a great Indian field hockey player.
  • He was born on 29 August 1905 in Allahabad in a Rajput family.
  • Dhyan Chand’s father Sameshwar Singh was in British India Army and Sharadha Singh was his mother.
  • He graduated from Victoria College in Gwalior.
  • Young chand loved wrestling in his childhood and started playing Hockey after joining the army at the age of 17.
  • Major Dhyan Chand earned three Olympic gold medals in 1928, 1932 and 1936.
  • He was known as “The Wizard” or “The Magician of Hockey” for his superb ball control.
  • August 29, Major Dhyan Chand’s birthday is celebrated as national sports day in India.
  • He was awarded India’s third highest civilian honour of “Padma Bhushan” in 1956 by the government of India.Recently, Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award is changed to Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award. It is India’s hightest sporting honour in the sports and games.
  • Major Dhyan Chand died on 3 December, 1979 at the age of 74 but people of all over the world will always remember him as “The Magician of Hockey”.
